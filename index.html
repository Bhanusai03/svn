About Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863â€“1902) was a spiritual leader, philosopher, and key figure in introducing Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. His inspiring personality and eloquent speeches brought global recognition to Indian culture and heritage.

Contributions

  • Promoted interfaith understanding and the idea of universal religion.
  • Established the Ramakrishna Mission to promote social welfare and spirituality.
  • Revitalized the Indian youth to embrace their heritage and contribute to the nation's progress.
  • Delivered a historic speech at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893.

Achievements

  • First Indian monk to represent Hinduism on a global stage.
  • Popularized the practice of yoga and meditation worldwide.
  • Inspired leaders and freedom fighters like Mahatma Gandhi and Subhas Chandra Bose.
  • Authored numerous works including "Raja Yoga" and "Karma Yoga."
